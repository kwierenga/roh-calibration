NA12878 chr22 robust crossover detection verdict
============================================================

Method: Mendelian-deterministic sites only (one parent het, other hom)
        Long-run threshold: 500 consecutive sites on both sides
        Interference filter: top 1 paternal, top 2 maternal by support score

Deterministic sites used: 20,671 paternal, 22,940 maternal

Raw candidates: 3 paternal, 3 maternal
Surviving interference filter: 1 paternal, 2 maternal

CAVEAT
------
This analysis is performed on the 1000G high-coverage release 20220422
phased VCF, which uses SHAPEIT-family phasing across the 3,202-sample
panel with trio constraints. The raw candidate count is high (5+5 with
MIN_RUN=500) vs. the deCODE-predicted ~1 expected for chr22 in a single
meiosis. The interference filter selects the top candidates by support
score, but cannot definitively distinguish:
  (a) real meiotic crossovers, and
  (b) parental-haplotype block-level label swaps in the released phasing.
Both produce identical trace signatures and identical Mendelian consistency.

Definitive validation requires one of:
  - Grandparent genotypes (CEPH 1463 has NA12877/NA12879/NA12889/NA12890;
    not present in 1000G 3,202-sample panel)
  - Independent re-phasing with strict trio mode (SHAPEIT5)
  - Platinum Genomes phased VCFs (Eberle 2017, 17-member pedigree phasing)
  - deCODE recombination map (Halldorsson 2019) as a Bayesian prior on position

Of these, Platinum Genomes is the lowest-friction next step.
